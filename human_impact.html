The Anthropocene Ocean

Understanding our footprint on the world's most vital ecosystem.

Plastic Pollution

An estimated 8 to 12 million metric tons of plastic enter the ocean every year. This exists as large debris leading to entanglement and drowning of seals and seabirds, and as **microplastics** that are ingested across the food web. The impact is systemic, affecting even the deepest trenches.

Microplastics absorb toxins from the water, posing a severe threat through bioaccumulation. Protecting our coasts starts with upstream waste management.

Climate Change & Acidification

Rising sea temperatures cause mass **coral bleaching**, destroying the crucial habitats that support a quarter of all marine life. Furthermore, excess CO2 absorption leads to **ocean acidification**, reducing the ability of shellfish and plankton to build their protective shells, impacting the entire oceanic food chain foundation.

This is a fundamental shift in ocean chemistry that requires global, coordinated emissions reduction efforts to reverse.

Overfishing & Destructive Practices

**Overfishing** depletes fish populations faster than they can recover, leading to ecological collapse. Destructive methods like **bottom trawling** physically obliterate deep-sea habitats. **Bycatch**—the accidental capture of non-target species—threatens vulnerable populations like dolphins and sea turtles.

Supporting sustainable aquaculture and enforcing international fishing limits are critical to restoring balance.
